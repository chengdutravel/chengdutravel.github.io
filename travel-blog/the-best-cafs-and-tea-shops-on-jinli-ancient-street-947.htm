The Best Cafés and Tea Shops on Jinli Ancient Street

  • Home
  • /
  • Travel Blog
  • /
  • Blog Details

Jinli Ancient Street, located in the heart of Chengdu, is a vibrant hub where history and modernity collide. Known for its traditional Sichuanese architecture, bustling markets, and mouthwatering street food, Jinli is also home to some of the most charming cafés and tea shops in the city. Whether you're a coffee enthusiast, a tea connoisseur, or simply looking for a cozy spot to unwind, Jinli’s eclectic mix of establishments offers something for everyone.

A Blend of Tradition and Modernity

Jinli Ancient Street is a living testament to Chengdu’s rich cultural heritage. The cobblestone pathways, red lanterns, and wooden storefronts transport visitors back in time, while the contemporary cafés and tea houses add a modern twist. This unique fusion makes Jinli a must-visit destination for travelers seeking both authenticity and comfort.

Why Jinli’s Cafés and Tea Shops Stand Out

Unlike generic coffee chains, the cafés and tea shops on Jinli Ancient Street often incorporate local flavors, traditional décor, and immersive experiences. From sipping premium Pu’er tea to enjoying a latte with a Sichuanese twist, every visit feels like a cultural exploration.

Top Cafés on Jinli Ancient Street

1. The Bamboo House Café

Nestled in a quiet corner of Jinli, The Bamboo House Café is a serene escape from the street’s lively atmosphere. The café’s interior features bamboo furnishings and soft traditional music, creating a tranquil ambiance.

Must-Try:
- Sichuan Spice Latte – A bold blend of espresso with a hint of Sichuan peppercorn for a tingling aftertaste.
- Matcha Bamboo Cake – A delicate dessert infused with green tea and served in bamboo-shaped molds.

2. Red Lantern Coffee

As the name suggests, this café is adorned with iconic red lanterns, making it a picturesque spot for photos. The menu combines Western coffee techniques with local ingredients.

Must-Try:
- Black Sesame Cappuccino – A creamy, nutty twist on the classic cappuccino.
- Osmanthus Cold Brew – A refreshing floral-infused coffee perfect for warm afternoons.

3. Jinli Roasters

For coffee purists, Jinli Roasters is a hidden gem. This specialty coffee shop sources beans from Yunnan and offers pour-over sessions where baristas explain the brewing process.

Must-Try:
- Yunnan Single-Origin Pour-Over – A smooth, fruity coffee with notes of citrus and cocoa.
- Sesame Coffee Bun – A crispy pastry paired perfectly with any espresso drink.

Best Tea Shops for an Authentic Experience

1. Tea Whisperer

This elegant tea house specializes in premium loose-leaf teas, with a focus on Sichuan’s famous teas like Mengding Ganlu and Zhuyeqing. The staff conducts traditional tea ceremonies, offering a deep dive into Chinese tea culture.

Must-Try:
- Mengding Ganlu (Sweet Dew Tea) – A light, sweet green tea with a lingering floral aroma.
- Jasmine Pearl Tea – Hand-rolled tea leaves that unfurl beautifully in hot water.

2. Ancient Aroma Tea House

With antique wooden tables and a courtyard filled with bonsai trees, Ancient Aroma feels like stepping into a bygone era. They serve rare aged Pu’er teas, some dating back decades.

Must-Try:
- 20-Year Aged Pu’er – A rich, earthy tea with complex layers of flavor.
- Ginger Honey Tea – A soothing blend ideal for cooler days.

3. Zen Tea Lounge

For those who prefer a modern twist, Zen Tea Lounge offers creative tea cocktails and fusion drinks. The minimalist décor and chill vibe make it a favorite among younger crowds.

Must-Try:
- Lychee Oolong Sparkler – A fizzy, fruity iced tea with a floral finish.
- Matcha Tiramisu – A dessert that blends Italian and Japanese influences.

Unique Features of Jinli’s Beverage Scene

Tea Tasting Workshops

Several tea shops, like Tea Whisperer, host workshops where visitors can learn about tea varieties, brewing techniques, and the history of Chinese tea. These sessions are both educational and interactive.

Themed Cafés with Cultural Twists

From calligraphy-themed coffee art to tea houses with live Guzheng performances, Jinli’s cafés and tea shops often incorporate cultural elements that enhance the overall experience.

Instagram-Worthy Spots

With their photogenic interiors—think hanging lanterns, bamboo gardens, and vintage tea sets—these establishments are perfect for capturing memorable travel moments.

Tips for Visiting Jinli’s Cafés and Tea Shops

  • Timing Matters: Mornings are quieter, while evenings bring a livelier crowd.
  • Cashless Convenience: Most places accept mobile payments like Alipay and WeChat Pay.
  • Seasonal Specials: Ask about limited-time drinks, especially during festivals like Chinese New Year.

Jinli Ancient Street is more than just a tourist attraction—it’s a sensory journey. Whether you’re savoring a meticulously brewed tea or enjoying a quirky coffee creation, the cafés and tea shops here promise an unforgettable experience.
