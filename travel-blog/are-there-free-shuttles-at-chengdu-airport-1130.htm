Are There Free Shuttles at Chengdu Airport?

  • Home
  • /
  • Travel Blog
  • /
  • Blog Details

Chengdu, the vibrant capital of Sichuan Province, is a gateway to China’s southwestern wonders. Whether you’re here for the spicy hotpot, the adorable giant pandas, or the breathtaking Jiuzhaigou Valley, your journey likely begins at Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport (CTU) or the newer Chengdu Tianfu International Airport (TFU). One of the most common questions travelers ask is: Are there free shuttles at Chengdu Airport? Let’s dive into everything you need to know about airport transportation in Chengdu.

Understanding Chengdu’s Airports

Before discussing shuttle services, it’s essential to clarify which airport you’re flying into. Chengdu has two major airports:

Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport (CTU)

Located about 16 km from the city center, Shuangliu is the older but more centrally located airport, handling most domestic and some international flights.

Chengdu Tianfu International Airport (TFU)

Opened in 2021, Tianfu is one of the largest airports in the world, situated approximately 50 km from downtown Chengdu. It serves as a hub for international and domestic flights, particularly those heading to Tibet and Western China.

Free Shuttle Services at Chengdu Airports

Now, let’s answer the big question: Are there free shuttles?

Free Inter-Terminal Shuttles (Between T1 and T2 at Shuangliu)

At Shuangliu Airport, if you need to transfer between Terminal 1 and Terminal 2, a free shuttle bus operates 24/7. The ride takes about 10 minutes and runs every 5-10 minutes, depending on the time of day.

Free Shuttles Between Shuangliu and Tianfu Airports

If you’re connecting between Shuangliu (CTU) and Tianfu (TFU), Chengdu offers a complimentary inter-airport shuttle for passengers with a valid same-day flight ticket. Here’s what you need to know:
- Operating Hours: 6:00 AM to 11:00 PM
- Frequency: Every 30-60 minutes
- Travel Time: Around 60-90 minutes, depending on traffic
- Where to Board: Look for signs directing you to the "Inter-Airport Shuttle" at both airports.

Free Hotel Shuttles

Many nearby hotels (especially those catering to international travelers) provide free shuttle services to and from the airports. If you’ve booked a hotel, check their website or contact them directly to confirm availability.

Paid Shuttle Alternatives

While free shuttles are limited, Chengdu offers several affordable and convenient paid options:

Airport Express Buses

Chengdu’s Airport Express Lines are a budget-friendly way to reach the city center or key districts.
- From Shuangliu Airport:
- Line 1: To downtown (Tianfu Square) – ¥10-15
- Line 2: To North Railway Station – ¥10-15
- From Tianfu Airport:
- Line 1: To Chunxi Road (shopping district) – ¥25-35
- Line 2: To Chengdu East Railway Station – ¥25-35

Metro (Subway)

Chengdu’s metro system is efficient and cheap:
- Shuangliu Airport: Connected via Line 10 (to downtown in ~30 minutes).
- Tianfu Airport: Served by Line 18 (to South Railway Station in ~45 minutes).

Taxi & Ride-Hailing

Taxis and ride-hailing apps (Didi) are widely available:
- Shuangliu to downtown: ~¥50-80
- Tianfu to downtown: ~¥150-200

Tips for Navigating Chengdu Airport Transportation

  1. Check Your Airport in Advance – Confirm whether you’re arriving at Shuangliu or Tianfu to avoid confusion.
  2. Download a Translation App – While signs are bilingual, having a translator helps with drivers or ticket purchases.
  3. Carry Small Bills – Some buses and taxis prefer cash.
  4. Allow Extra Time for Tianfu – Due to its distance, factor in longer travel times.

Exploring Chengdu Beyond the Airport

Once you’ve figured out your transportation, Chengdu awaits with unforgettable experiences:
- Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding – Meet China’s national treasure.
- Jinli Ancient Street – A foodie paradise with Sichuan snacks.
- Leshan Giant Buddha – A short trip from Chengdu, this UNESCO site is awe-inspiring.

Whether you’re relying on free shuttles or opting for paid transit, Chengdu’s airports are well-connected, ensuring a smooth start to your Sichuan adventure.
