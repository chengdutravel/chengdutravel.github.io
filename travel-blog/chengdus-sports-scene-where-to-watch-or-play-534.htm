Chengdu’s Sports Scene: Where to Watch or Play

  • Home
  • /
  • Travel Blog
  • /
  • Blog Details

Chengdu, a city known for its spicy hotpot, adorable pandas, and laid-back teahouse culture, is also a hidden gem for sports enthusiasts. Whether you’re a spectator craving the electric atmosphere of a live match or an active traveler looking to break a sweat, Chengdu offers a vibrant sports scene that blends modern facilities with traditional Chinese athleticism. From world-class stadiums to grassroots basketball courts, here’s your ultimate guide to experiencing sports in Chengdu.

Spectator Sports: Where to Catch the Action

Football Fever at Chengdu Phoenix Hill Sports Park

Football (soccer) is wildly popular in Chengdu, and the city’s passion for the game is on full display at the Chengdu Phoenix Hill Sports Park. Home to the Chengdu Rongcheng F.C., this state-of-the-art stadium hosts electrifying matches in the Chinese Super League. The local fans, known for their boisterous chants and unwavering loyalty, create an atmosphere that rivals European football culture. Pro tip: Grab a ticket early—matches often sell out, especially when rivals like Beijing Guoan or Shanghai SIPG come to town.

Ping-Pong (Table Tennis) at Sichuan Provincial Gymnasium

Table tennis isn’t just China’s national sport—it’s a way of life. For a taste of elite-level play, head to the Sichuan Provincial Gymnasium, where regional and national tournaments are frequently held. Even if you’re not catching a pro match, the gymnasium’s public tables are a great place to witness locals, from kids to retirees, showcasing lightning-fast reflexes.

Basketball at Wuliangye Chengdu Sports Center

Basketball has exploded in popularity across China, and Chengdu is no exception. The Wuliangye Chengdu Sports Center regularly hosts CBA (Chinese Basketball Association) games, featuring the Sichuan Blue Whales. The energy here is contagious, with fans cheering for slam dunks and three-pointers. Off-season? Join pickup games at outdoor courts in parks like Tianfu Square or near Sichuan University.

Active Sports: Where to Play Like a Local

Running and Cycling Along the Jinjiang River Greenway

For fitness lovers, the Jinjiang River Greenway is a scenic escape from the urban hustle. This 10-kilometer stretch offers dedicated lanes for runners and cyclists, with lush greenery and occasional street performers adding to the vibe. Early mornings are ideal—join locals practicing tai chi or groups of runners training for marathons like the Chengdu International Marathon.

Badminton at Shuangliu Sports Center

Badminton is a Chengdu favorite, and the Shuangliu Sports Center is one of the best places to play. With affordable court rentals and a lively community, it’s easy to find a match. Don’t have a racket? No problem—many venues offer rentals for a small fee.

Swimming at Chengdu Olympic Sports Center

Beat the heat at the Chengdu Olympic Sports Center’s indoor and outdoor pools. The facility is Olympic-standard, attracting both casual swimmers and serious athletes. For a more relaxed dip, check out rooftop pools at luxury hotels like The Ritz-Carlton, which offer skyline views.

Unique Sports Experiences

Sichuan Opera Face-Changing Performances (Okay, Not a Sport—But Close!)

While not a traditional sport, Sichuan Opera’s face-changing (bian lian) performances require athleticism and precision. The rapid mask swaps, combined with acrobatics, are a must-see. Venues like Shufeng Yayun Teahouse offer nightly shows where you can marvel at this intangible cultural heritage.

Mahjong: Chengdu’s Favorite Mind Sport

Mahjong is Chengdu’s unofficial national pastime. Head to a local teahouse or park, and you’ll find groups huddled over tiles, strategizing like chess masters. Some venues even offer beginner lessons—just don’t bet unless you’re ready to lose (the locals are good).

Sports Bars and Fan Zones

The Brews & Balls at Craft’d Union

For expats and travelers craving a taste of home, Craft’d Union is Chengdu’s go-to sports bar. With big screens broadcasting Premier League, NBA, and UFC matches, it’s the perfect spot to grab a craft beer and cheer alongside fellow fans.

Local Fan Zones Near Chunxi Road

On game days, Chunxi Road’s outdoor screens transform into impromptu fan zones. Street vendors sell skewers and beer while crowds gather to watch major events like the World Cup or Asian Games. It’s a rowdy, unforgettable way to experience Chengdu’s sports culture.

Final Tips for Sports Travelers

  • Timing Matters: Check schedules for local leagues—many events align with national holidays.
  • Gear Up: For outdoor activities, pack breathable clothing; Chengdu’s humidity can be intense.
  • Language Hack: Learn phrases like “Jiayou!” (加油, “Let’s go!”) to blend in with cheering crowds.

Whether you’re here to watch, play, or simply soak up the energy, Chengdu’s sports scene is a thrilling addition to any travel itinerary. So lace up those sneakers or grab a seat in the stands—adventure awaits!
