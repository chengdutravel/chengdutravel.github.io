Hostels in Chengdu with Outdoor Seating and Gardens

  • Home
  • /

  • /

Chengdu, the capital of Sichuan Province, is a city that effortlessly blends modernity with tradition. Known for its spicy cuisine, relaxed teahouse culture, and adorable giant pandas, it’s no surprise that travelers flock here year-round. For backpackers and budget-conscious travelers, hostels are often the go-to accommodation—but not all hostels are created equal. If you’re looking for a place where you can unwind in nature without leaving the city, these Chengdu hostels with outdoor seating and gardens are perfect for you.

Why Choose a Hostel with Outdoor Spaces in Chengdu?

Chengdu’s climate is relatively mild, making it ideal for outdoor relaxation. Whether you want to sip tea under the shade of bamboo, mingle with fellow travelers in a lush courtyard, or simply enjoy the fresh air after a day of exploring, a hostel with a garden or outdoor seating can elevate your stay.

Beyond comfort, these hostels often reflect Chengdu’s laid-back vibe, offering a peaceful retreat from the bustling city. Many also host events like BBQ nights, live music, or yoga sessions in their gardens, creating a social yet serene atmosphere.

Top Hostels in Chengdu with Outdoor Seating and Gardens

1. Lazybones Hostel

Location: Near Wuhou Temple

Lazybones Hostel is a favorite among backpackers for its cozy atmosphere and beautiful courtyard. The outdoor area is filled with bamboo plants, wooden benches, and string lights that create a magical ambiance at night.

What Makes It Special?

  • A spacious garden with hammocks and picnic tables.
  • Regular outdoor movie nights and live music events.
  • A communal kitchen where guests can cook and dine al fresco.

2. Sleepy Panda Hostel

Location: Close to Kuanzhai Alley

True to its name, Sleepy Panda Hostel embraces Chengdu’s panda culture with playful decor and a tranquil garden. The outdoor seating area is perfect for morning coffee or evening beers.

Highlights:

  • A rooftop garden with panoramic city views.
  • Free tai chi sessions in the courtyard.
  • A mini-library where you can read surrounded by greenery.

3. Mingtown Emei Hostel

Location: Near Jinli Ancient Street

Mingtown Emei combines traditional Sichuan architecture with modern hostel amenities. Its courtyard, adorned with lanterns and wooden carvings, feels like stepping into old Chengdu.

Why Stay Here?

  • A serene garden with koi ponds and stone pathways.
  • Outdoor tea ceremonies hosted weekly.
  • Private bamboo huts for those who want extra privacy.

4. The Loft Hostel

Location: In the heart of Chengdu’s nightlife district

The Loft is a social hub with a rooftop garden that doubles as a bar. It’s perfect for travelers who want to meet new people while enjoying Chengdu’s skyline.

Standout Features:

  • A rooftop BBQ area with city views.
  • Outdoor yoga sessions at sunrise.
  • Live DJ nights under the stars.

5. Garden Inn Hostel

Location: Near People’s Park

As the name suggests, Garden Inn Hostel is all about greenery. The entire property feels like a mini botanical garden, with flowers, bonsai trees, and wooden decks.

What to Expect:

  • A quiet garden with meditation corners.
  • Outdoor calligraphy workshops.
  • Freshly brewed tea served in the courtyard daily.

How to Make the Most of Your Stay

Staying in a hostel with outdoor spaces is more than just having a place to sleep—it’s about embracing Chengdu’s culture. Here’s how to enhance your experience:

Join Outdoor Activities

Many hostels organize events like group hikes, garden parties, or Sichuan cooking classes. Don’t hesitate to sign up—it’s a great way to meet people.

Try Local Tea in the Garden

Chengdu is famous for its teahouses, but why not enjoy tea right in your hostel’s garden? Some places even offer complimentary tea sets.

Work Remotely in Nature

If you’re a digital nomad, hostels with gardens provide a refreshing alternative to stuffy co-working spaces.

Capture the Aesthetic

These hostels are Instagram-worthy. Whether it’s the lantern-lit courtyards or rooftop sunsets, you’ll leave with stunning photos.

Final Tips for Booking

  • Check Seasonal Availability: Some gardens are more enjoyable in spring and autumn.
  • Read Reviews: Look for mentions of cleanliness and outdoor maintenance.
  • Ask About Events: Some hostels only advertise activities upon arrival.

Chengdu’s hostels with outdoor seating and gardens offer a unique way to experience the city. Whether you’re a solo traveler, a couple, or a group of friends, these spots provide comfort, beauty, and a touch of local charm.
