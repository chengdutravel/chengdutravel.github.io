The Ultimate Guide to Getting to and from Chengdu Airport

  • Home
  • /
  • Travel Blog
  • /

Welcome to Chengdu, the gateway to Sichuan and the home of the giant panda! Whether you're here for the spicy hotpot, the ancient history, or as a launchpad for adventures to Jiuzhaigou or Tibet, your journey begins at Chengdu's primary airport. Navigating a new city's transport can be daunting, but fear not. This ultimate guide will break down every option for getting to and from Chengdu Tianfu International Airport (TFU) and Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport (CTU), helping you choose the perfect route for your style, budget, and itinerary.

Know Your Airports: Shuangliu vs. Tianfu

First things first: Chengdu is unique as it operates two major international airports. Knowing which one you're flying into or out of is crucial.

Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport (CTU)

The veteran. Shuangliu has been Chengdu's main airport for decades and is located much closer to the city center, about 16km (10 miles) southwest. It handles a significant portion of domestic flights and some international routes. Its proximity makes it a quicker trip downtown.

Chengdu Tianfu International Airport (TFU)

The new superstar. Opened in 2021, Tianfu is a massive, state-of-the-art hub located about 50km (31 miles) southeast of the city center. It's designed to be a major aviation gateway for western China and is increasingly handling most international and many domestic flights. Always, always double-check your ticket for the airport code (CTU vs. TFU).

Mastering the Journey from Chengdu Shuangliu Airport (CTU)

Given its central location, options from Shuangliu are fast and varied.

Metro Line 10: The Speed Demon

This is arguably the best way to get downtown. The airport station is connected to Terminal 2. Line 10 zips you directly to key interchange hubs like Taipingyuan Station (where you can switch to Line 3, 7, 9) and into the city center in about 35-45 minutes. It's affordable, avoids traffic, and runs frequently. Purchase a ticket from the automated machines or use a mobile payment app like Alipay/WeChat to scan into the gates.

Taxi & Ride-Hailing (Didi)

Taxis are readily available outside both terminals. A ride to the city center (e.g., Jinli Ancient Street, Chunxi Road) takes 30-50 minutes depending on traffic and costs between ¥50-¥100. For a more seamless experience, use the Didi app (China's Uber). You can set your destination in English, pay via the app, and avoid language barriers. Designated pick-up points are well-signed.

Airport Shuttle Buses

Several shuttle bus lines connect CTU to major points like the North Railway Station, Chunxi Road, and others. They are cost-effective (around ¥15-20) but are subject to Chengdu's infamous traffic. Good for travelers with light luggage heading to specific terminals.

Conquering the Distance from Chengdu Tianfu Airport (TFU)

Don't let the distance from Tianfu intimidate you. The city has built impressive infrastructure to connect it.

The Tianfu Airport Express (Metro Line 18)

This is your go-to for efficiency. Line 18 is a modern, high-speed subway line dedicated to connecting Tianfu to the urban core. It has two types of services: Express (stops at major stations only) and Regular. The express train can get you to South Railway Station (Nanzhan) in about 30-35 minutes! From there, you can transfer to the extensive metro network. It's clean, fast, and incredibly reliable.

Airport Shuttle Buses to Key Destinations

Tianfu operates an extensive network of shuttle buses to locations across Chengdu and even to other cities in Sichuan. Lines go to Chunxi Road, East Railway Station, Shuangliu Airport, and major bus terminals. While slower than the metro due to road traffic, they are direct and comfortable, costing approximately ¥25-40. Perfect if your hotel is near one of their stops.

Taxi, Didi, and Private Transfers

This is the most convenient but also the most expensive option. A taxi or Didi from TFU to the city center can take 60-90 minutes and cost ¥150-¥250. For groups or those with a lot of luggage, it can be worth it. For a stress-free arrival, especially late at night, consider pre-booking a private transfer. Many hotels also offer airport pickup services for guests—inquire when booking!

High-Speed Rail: The Future is Now

Chengdu has integrated high-speed rail directly into Tianfu Airport. You can catch trains from the airport's station to destinations like Chengdu South Railway Station (in about 20 minutes) and Chengdu East Railway Station. This is a fantastic option if you're continuing your journey beyond Chengdu immediately, say, to Leshan for the Giant Buddha or to Chongqing.

Pro Tips for the Savvy Traveler

Navigating with Technology

Your phone is your best friend. Download Amap or Baidu Maps (available in English) for real-time navigation and transit directions. Have Didi installed and set up with payment before you arrive. While signs are in English, translation apps can help with any confusion.

Money and Payments

While airport services accept cash, China is a cashless society. Set up Alipay or WeChat Pay on your phone and link an international credit card. You can use these to pay for metro tickets, Didi rides, and even at convenience stores for water. It’s a game-changer.

Luggage Logistics & Connectivity

Both airports offer luggage storage services if you have a long layover and want to explore. Also, purchase a local SIM card or arrange an e-SIM for data immediately upon arrival—it’s essential for using maps and ride-hailing apps. Free airport Wi-Fi often requires a Chinese phone number for verification, which can be a catch-22 for new arrivals.

Timing is Everything

  • Rush Hour: Chengdu's traffic peaks from 7:30-9:30 AM and 5:00-7:30 PM. Avoid road-based transport during these times if you're on a tight schedule.
  • Late-Night Arrivals: If your flight lands after midnight, the metro will likely be closed. Your best bets are pre-booked transfers, taxis, or Didi. Shuttle buses may have limited schedules.

Beyond the Airport: Making Chengdu Your Base

Your transport choice might depend on your next adventure. If you're heading straight to Panda Base, a taxi from either airport might be most direct. If you're catching a train to Xi'an or Lhasa from Chengdu East Station, the Line 18 metro to South Station and then a transfer might be ideal. For the foodie heading to Kuanzhai Alley, the metro offers a drop-off right in the action.

The key to a smooth start in Chengdu is preparation. Confirm your airport, download the essential apps, have a digital payment method ready, and choose the transport mode that matches your priorities—speed, cost, or comfort. Chengdu is a vibrant, welcoming city, and its modern airport connections are your first step into the land of pandas, peppercorns, and profound culture. So take a deep breath, grab your bags, and get ready for an unforgettable Sichuan adventure. Your journey, from touchdown to hotpot, starts now.
