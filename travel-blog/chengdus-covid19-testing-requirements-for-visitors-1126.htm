Chengdu’s COVID-19 Testing Requirements for Visitors

  • Home
  • /
  • Travel Blog
  • /
  • Blog Details

Chengdu, the vibrant capital of Sichuan Province, is a top destination for travelers seeking spicy hotpot, adorable giant pandas, and rich cultural heritage. However, as the world continues to navigate the post-pandemic era, entry requirements—especially COVID-19 testing protocols—remain a key consideration for visitors. Whether you’re planning to explore Jinli Ancient Street or marvel at the Leshan Giant Buddha, understanding Chengdu’s current testing rules is essential for a smooth trip.

Current COVID-19 Testing Policies for Entry

As of 2023, Chengdu follows China’s dynamic "Zero-COVID" strategy, which means regulations can change rapidly based on case numbers. Here’s what you need to know before booking your flight:

Pre-Departure Testing

Most international travelers must present a negative PCR test result taken within 48 hours before boarding their flight to China. Some airlines may require additional rapid antigen tests (RATs) at check-in, so always verify with your carrier.

On-Arrival Testing

Upon landing at Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport or Tianfu International Airport, travelers may undergo:
- Mandatory PCR testing at the airport (free of charge).
- Health declaration forms via the China Customs Health QR Code (filled online or on paper).
- Possible quarantine if you test positive or come from a high-risk region.

Domestic Travelers: Inter-Provincial Rules

If you’re arriving from another Chinese city, requirements vary:
- Green Health Code (via Sichuan’s local health app) is typically required.
- Some provinces may require 24- or 48-hour negative test results, especially if cases are rising.

Navigating Chengdu’s Health QR Codes

A key part of travel in Chengdu is managing health status through QR codes. Here’s how it works:

Sichuan Health Code (四川天府健康通)

  1. Download the app or access it via WeChat/Alipay.
  2. Register with your passport/ID and phone number.
  3. A green code grants access to public spaces; yellow/red may require quarantine.

Travel Record QR

Some attractions (e.g., Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding) may ask for a 14-day travel history scan. Keep screenshots handy if internet access is spotty.

Testing Facilities for Tourists in Chengdu

If you need a PCR test during your stay, Chengdu offers convenient options:
- Hospitals: West China Hospital, Chengdu No. 7 People’s Hospital (English-friendly).
- Clinics: International SOS Chengdu.
- Airport Testing: Available for departing travelers (fee applies).

Tip: Results usually take 6–12 hours. Plan tests ahead of flights or event bookings.

How Testing Rules Affect Your Itinerary

Attractions & Dining

Most venues no longer require negative tests, but always check:
- Panda Base: May request a green code +体温检测 (temperature check).
- Hotpot Restaurants: Crowded spots like Xiaolongkan might enforce stricter checks.

Public Transport

  • Subways/Buses: Masks are encouraged; health codes rarely checked.
  • Taxis/Ride-hailing: Drivers may decline passengers with yellow/red codes.

What If You Test Positive?

Chengdu’s quarantine policies are strict but efficient:
- Asymptomatic/mild cases: 7-day home/hotel quarantine (with monitoring).
- Severe cases: Transfer to designated hospitals (e.g., Chengdu Public Health Center).
- Close contacts: Often 5-day home isolation + repeated testing.

Pro tip: Travel insurance covering COVID-19 treatment is highly recommended.

The Future of Travel in Chengdu

While testing rules are easing, flexibility remains crucial. Follow Chengdu Government’s official WeChat account or consult your hotel for real-time updates. With proper prep, you can sip tea in a 茶馆 (cháguǎn) or hike Qingcheng Mountain without a hitch—just keep that health code green!

Bonus: For hassle-free travel, pack extra masks and a portable charger (QR codes drain batteries fast!). Now, go enjoy that 麻辣 (málà) flavor Chengdu is famous for!
