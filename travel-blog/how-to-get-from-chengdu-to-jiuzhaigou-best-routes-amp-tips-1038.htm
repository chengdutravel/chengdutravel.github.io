How to Get from Chengdu to Jiuzhaigou: Best Routes & Tips

  • Home
  • /
  • Travel Blog
  • /
  • Blog Details

Jiuzhaigou Valley, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is one of China’s most breathtaking natural wonders. Known for its turquoise lakes, cascading waterfalls, and snow-capped peaks, it’s a must-visit for nature lovers. If you're starting your journey in Chengdu, getting to Jiuzhaigou requires some planning. Here’s a detailed guide on the best routes, travel tips, and insider advice to make your trip smooth and enjoyable.

By Air: The Fastest Option

For travelers short on time, flying is the quickest way to reach Jiuzhaigou from Chengdu.

Chengdu to Jiuzhai Huanglong Airport (JZH)

Jiuzhai Huanglong Airport is the closest airport to Jiuzhaigou, located about 88 kilometers (55 miles) away. Several airlines operate daily flights from Chengdu Shuangliu International Airport (CTU) or Chengdu Tianfu International Airport (TFU).

  • Flight Duration: Approximately 1 hour.
  • Airlines: Sichuan Airlines, Air China, and China Eastern.
  • Best Booking Tip: Book early, especially during peak seasons (April–November), as flights fill up quickly.

From the Airport to Jiuzhaigou Valley

Once you land, you’ll need ground transportation to reach Jiuzhaigou National Park:
- Airport Shuttle Buses: Available outside the terminal, taking about 1.5–2 hours.
- Private Taxis: More expensive but faster and more comfortable.
- Rental Cars: Possible but not recommended due to mountainous roads.

Pro Tip: If you’re prone to altitude sickness, take it slow—Jiuzhaigou sits at around 2,000–3,000 meters (6,500–9,800 feet) above sea level.

By Bus: The Budget-Friendly Choice

If you prefer a scenic and economical journey, taking a bus from Chengdu to Jiuzhaigou is a great option.

Chengdu Chadianzi Bus Station to Jiuzhaigou

Long-distance buses depart daily from Chengdu’s Chadianzi Bus Station.

  • Duration: 8–10 hours (depending on traffic and road conditions).
  • Ticket Price: Around ¥150–¥200 (varies by season).
  • Departure Times: Early morning (6:00 AM–8:00 AM) for daytime travel.

What to Expect on the Bus Ride

The route passes through stunning landscapes, including the Minjiang River Valley and Tibetan villages. However, the roads can be winding, so motion sickness medication might help.

Travel Hack: Opt for a sleeper bus if available—it’s more comfortable for overnight trips.

By Private Car or Tour: Flexibility & Comfort

For those who value convenience and flexibility, hiring a private car or joining a guided tour is ideal.

Renting a Car

  • Pros: Freedom to stop at scenic spots like Huanglong National Park.
  • Cons: Mountain driving can be challenging for inexperienced drivers.
  • Estimated Cost: ¥1,500–¥2,500 for a round trip.

Joining a Guided Tour

Many tour operators in Chengdu offer 2–3 day Jiuzhaigou packages, including transportation, accommodation, and park tickets.

  • Recommended Tours: Look for small-group tours for a more personalized experience.
  • Bonus: Guides often share insider knowledge about hidden trails and photo spots.

Best Time to Visit Jiuzhaigou

While Jiuzhaigou is stunning year-round, each season offers a unique experience:

  • Spring (April–June): Blooming flowers and mild weather.
  • Summer (July–August): Lush greenery but crowded.
  • Autumn (September–October): Peak season with vibrant fall foliage.
  • Winter (November–March): Fewer tourists and frozen waterfalls.

Avoid: Chinese national holidays (October 1–7) when the park is extremely crowded.

Essential Travel Tips for Jiuzhaigou

Packing Checklist

  • Layered Clothing: Temperatures vary drastically between day and night.
  • Comfortable Hiking Shoes: The park involves a lot of walking.
  • Altitude Sickness Pills: Just in case.
  • Camera or Smartphone: You’ll want to capture every moment.

Park Entry & Navigation

  • Ticket Price: ¥169 (off-season) to ¥259 (peak season).
  • Shuttle Buses Inside the Park: Highly recommended to save time.
  • Must-See Spots: Five-Color Pond, Nuorilang Waterfall, and Long Lake.

Local Etiquette & Safety

  • Respect local Tibetan culture—avoid touching prayer flags or religious artifacts.
  • Stay on marked trails to protect the fragile ecosystem.

Alternative Routes: Combining with Other Destinations

If you have extra time, consider these detours:

Adding Huanglong National Park

Famous for its travertine pools, Huanglong is a 3-hour drive from Jiuzhaigou. Many tours include both parks.

Exploring Songpan Ancient Town

A historic Tibetan town en route, perfect for a short stop to experience local culture.

Final Thoughts on Transportation Choices

Each travel option has its perks—whether you prioritize speed, budget, or adventure. Plan ahead, book early, and prepare for an unforgettable journey into one of China’s most magical landscapes.

Now that you know how to get from Chengdu to Jiuzhaigou, all that’s left is to pack your bags and embark on your adventure!
