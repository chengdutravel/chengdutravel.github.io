Chengdu’s Best Family-Friendly Photography Spots

  • Home
  • /
  • Travel Blog
  • /
  • Blog Details

Chengdu, the capital of Sichuan Province, is a city that effortlessly blends modernity with tradition. Known for its spicy cuisine, laid-back teahouse culture, and of course, the adorable giant pandas, it’s also a paradise for photography enthusiasts—especially families looking to capture unforgettable moments. Whether you’re snapping candid shots of your kids playing or posing against stunning backdrops, Chengdu offers countless picturesque locations. Here’s a curated list of the best family-friendly photography spots in Chengdu.

1. Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding

Why It’s Perfect for Families

No trip to Chengdu is complete without visiting the Chengdu Panda Base. This conservation center is home to dozens of giant pandas, red pandas, and other wildlife. The lush bamboo forests and open enclosures provide a natural, photogenic setting.

Best Photo Opportunities

  • Panda Playtime: Capture your kids’ reactions as they watch pandas munching on bamboo or tumbling around.
  • Red Panda Walkway: These smaller, raccoon-like creatures often roam freely on elevated paths, offering unique close-up shots.
  • Panda Sculpture Garden: A great spot for fun family photos with panda statues of all sizes.

Pro Tip

Visit early in the morning (around 8 AM) when the pandas are most active. The soft morning light also makes for better photos.

2. Jinli Ancient Street

Why It’s Perfect for Families

Jinli Street is a bustling pedestrian lane that recreates the charm of ancient Sichuan. With its red lanterns, traditional architecture, and street food stalls, it’s a vibrant backdrop for family photos.

Best Photo Opportunities

  • Lantern Alley: The rows of hanging red lanterns create a magical atmosphere, especially at dusk.
  • Street Performers: Musicians and face-changing (Bian Lian) artists add dynamic energy to your shots.
  • Local Snacks: Candid shots of kids trying tanghulu (candied fruit) or spicy skewers make for adorable keepsakes.

Pro Tip

Wear colorful clothing to stand out against the earthy tones of the ancient buildings.

3. People’s Park (Renmin Park)

Why It’s Perfect for Families

This urban oasis is where locals gather to dance, play mahjong, and sip tea. The park’s serene lakes, bridges, and gardens offer a relaxed setting for family portraits.

Best Photo Opportunities

  • Teahouse Culture: Snap photos of your family enjoying tea in a traditional bamboo chair.
  • Boat Rides: Rent a paddleboat on the lake for playful shots with the city skyline in the background.
  • Matchmaking Corner: While not for kids, the quirky “marriage market” signs make for amusing background details.

Pro Tip

Visit on a weekday to avoid crowds and get cleaner shots of the scenic spots.

4. Wide and Narrow Alleys (Kuanzhai Xiangzi)

Why It’s Perfect for Families

This restored Qing Dynasty neighborhood combines history with trendy cafés and souvenir shops. The mix of old and new creates a visually rich environment.

Best Photo Opportunities

  • Courtyard Houses: The intricate wooden doors and stone pathways are perfect for vintage-style portraits.
  • Street Art: Murals and installations add a modern twist to your photos.
  • Evening Lights: As the sun sets, the lanterns and string lights illuminate the alleys beautifully.

Pro Tip

Dress in matching outfits for a cohesive family album look.

5. Chengdu Happy Valley

Why It’s Perfect for Families

For families with older kids, this amusement park offers thrilling rides and colorful attractions. The vibrant scenery is ideal for action shots and candid laughter.

Best Photo Opportunities

  • Roller Coasters: Freeze-frame the excitement with mid-ride expressions.
  • Carousel: The classic spinning ride provides a dreamy, nostalgic backdrop.
  • Seasonal Decor: The park goes all out for holidays like Halloween and Christmas.

Pro Tip

Use burst mode to capture fast-moving rides without blur.

6. Donghu Park (East Lake Park)

Why It’s Perfect for Families

Less touristy than People’s Park, Donghu Park features expansive greenery, a tranquil lake, and artistic installations. It’s ideal for a peaceful family photoshoot.

Best Photo Opportunities

  • Lotus Pond: In summer, the blooming flowers create a stunning natural frame.
  • Swing Bridges: Playful shots of kids crossing the wobbly bridges add movement to your album.
  • Modern Sculptures: The park’s contemporary art pieces offer unique backdrops.

Pro Tip

Bring a picnic blanket for relaxed, candid shots on the grass.

7. Taikoo Li (Sino-Ocean Taikoo Li)

Why It’s Perfect for Families

This upscale shopping district blends traditional Sichuan architecture with luxury brands. The sleek design and open plazas are great for stylish family photos.

Best Photo Opportunities

  • Water Features: The reflective pools create mirror-like effects for artistic shots.
  • Luxury Storefronts: The high-end boutiques provide a chic urban backdrop.
  • Nighttime Ambiance: The area lights up beautifully after dark.

Pro Tip

Visit during golden hour for soft, flattering lighting.

8. Qingcheng Mountain

Why It’s Perfect for Families

A short trip from Chengdu, this UNESCO-listed mountain is a serene escape with lush forests, Taoist temples, and misty peaks.

Best Photo Opportunities

  • Cable Car Rides: Aerial shots of the family with panoramic mountain views.
  • Ancient Temples: The red-and-gold structures contrast beautifully with the greenery.
  • Waterfalls: Long-exposure shots of cascading water create a dreamy effect.

Pro Tip

Pack a tripod for stable group photos in low-light temple areas.

9. Chengdu Zoo

Why It’s Perfect for Families

Smaller and less crowded than the Panda Base, the Chengdu Zoo houses diverse animals, making it a fun alternative for younger kids.

Best Photo Opportunities

  • Elephant Enclosure: The gentle giants often interact with visitors.
  • Butterfly House: Vibrant wings fluttering around make for magical close-ups.
  • Playground Areas: Candid shots of kids running and laughing.

Pro Tip

Use a zoom lens to capture animal details without disturbing them.

10. Anshun Bridge & Hejiang Pavilion

Why It’s Perfect for Families

This riverside area offers stunning views of the Jinjiang River, especially at sunset. The blend of modern bridges and historic pavilions is visually striking.

Best Photo Opportunities

  • Golden Hour: The warm light reflecting off the water enhances portraits.
  • Bridge Silhouettes: Dramatic architectural shots with the city skyline.
  • River Cruises: Capture the family on a boat with Chengdu’s lights twinkling behind.

Pro Tip

Use a polarizing filter to reduce glare on the water.

From pandas to ancient streets, Chengdu’s diverse scenery ensures every family photo tells a story. Whether you’re a professional photographer or just snapping memories with your phone, these spots guarantee frame-worthy moments.
