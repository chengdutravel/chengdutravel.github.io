Weekend in Chengdu: A Romantic Itinerary for Couples

  • Home
  • /
  • Travel Blog
  • /
  • Blog Details

Chengdu, the capital of Sichuan Province, is a city that effortlessly blends ancient charm with modern vibrancy. Known for its spicy cuisine, laid-back teahouse culture, and adorable giant pandas, it’s also a perfect destination for couples seeking a romantic getaway. Whether you’re strolling through lush gardens, savoring fiery hotpot, or cuddling up in a cozy boutique hotel, Chengdu offers endless opportunities for love to blossom. Here’s how to make the most of a romantic weekend in this captivating city.

Day 1: A Slow Start with Pandas and Tea

Morning: Meet the Giant Pandas

No trip to Chengdu is complete without visiting the Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding. Arrive early to avoid crowds and catch the pandas at their most active—during feeding time. Watching these adorable creatures munch on bamboo is a heartwarming experience you’ll cherish as a couple. Don’t miss the red pandas (smaller and equally cute) in the same park.

Pro tip: Book a private tour or a “panda keeper” experience for a more intimate encounter.

Late Morning: A Leisurely Brunch at The Temple House

After your panda adventure, head to The Temple House, a luxurious boutique hotel in the heart of Taikoo Li. Their restaurant offers a mix of Western and Sichuan-inspired brunch dishes. Try the dan dan noodles or eggs Benedict with a local twist. The serene courtyard is perfect for a quiet moment together.

Afternoon: Teahouse Culture at People’s Park

For an authentic Chengdu experience, spend your afternoon at People’s Park (Renmin Park). Find a spot at Heming Teahouse, one of the city’s oldest, and sip on fragrant jasmine tea while listening to live traditional music. If you’re feeling adventurous, try the ear-cleaning service—a quirky but relaxing local tradition.

Stroll hand-in-hand through the park’s gardens, rent a paddleboat on the lake, or simply people-watch. The slow pace here is a reminder to savor every moment together.

Evening: Hotpot Date at Long Senyuan

As the sun sets, dive into Chengdu’s culinary soul with a romantic hotpot dinner. Long Senyuan is a local favorite, offering a less touristy atmosphere. Opt for a split pot (yuanyang guo)—half spicy, half mild—to suit both your palates. Pair it with chilled plum wine for a perfect balance.

After dinner, take a moonlit walk along Jinli Ancient Street. The lantern-lit alleyways and traditional architecture create a dreamy backdrop for romance.

Day 2: Culture, Shopping, and Nightlife

Morning: A Spiritual Start at Wenshu Monastery

Begin your day at Wenshu Monastery, a peaceful Buddhist temple surrounded by lush greenery. Light incense together and make a wish for your future. The monastery’s vegetarian restaurant serves delicious Buddhist cuisine—try their mock-meat dishes, which are surprisingly flavorful.

Late Morning: Creative Vibes at Kuanzhai Alley

Next, explore Kuanzhai Alley (Wide and Narrow Alley), a beautifully restored historic area. The mix of Qing Dynasty architecture and trendy boutiques makes it ideal for couples who love culture and shopping. Stop by a calligraphy shop to get your names written in Chinese characters as a unique souvenir.

Afternoon: Art and Coffee at Fangsuo Bookstore

For a quieter interlude, visit Fangsuo Bookstore in Taikoo Li. This stunning bookstore doubles as a cultural space with art exhibitions and a cozy café. Grab a coffee and lose yourselves in the shelves of beautifully curated books.

Evening: Rooftop Drinks at NICCOLO

As night falls, head to the rooftop bar at NICCOLO Hotel for cocktails with a view. The glittering skyline of Chengdu is the perfect setting for a toast to your time together. Try their signature “Sichuan Mule,” a spicy twist on the classic Moscow Mule.

Night: Jazz and Cocktails at JING Bar

End your weekend with live jazz at JING Bar in The Temple House. The sultry music and intimate ambiance make it a favorite among couples. Sip on craft cocktails infused with Sichuan peppercorns for a final taste of the local flavor.

Romantic Stays: Where to Rest Your Heads

For an extra-special touch, book a room at one of Chengdu’s most romantic hotels:
- The Temple House: Blending traditional Sichuan architecture with modern luxury.
- NICCOLO Chengdu: Sleek and stylish with breathtaking city views.
- Chengdu Marriott Hotel Financial Center: Offers spacious suites and a serene spa for couples.

Whether you’re bonding over pandas, sharing a spicy hotpot, or toasting to love under the stars, Chengdu is a city that will leave you both with unforgettable memories.
