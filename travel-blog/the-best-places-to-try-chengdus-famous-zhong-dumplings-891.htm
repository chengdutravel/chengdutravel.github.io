The Best Places to Try Chengdu’s Famous Zhong Dumplings

  • /
  • Travel Blog
  • /
  • Blog Details

Chengdu, the capital of Sichuan Province, is a paradise for food lovers. Among its many culinary delights, Zhong dumplings (钟水饺) stand out as a must-try dish. These delicate, savory dumplings are a local specialty, featuring a unique combination of flavors—spicy, sweet, and umami—that define Sichuan cuisine. If you're planning a trip to Chengdu or simply want to explore its food scene, here’s a guide to the best places to savor authentic Zhong dumplings.

What Makes Zhong Dumplings Special?

Unlike typical dumplings found elsewhere, Zhong dumplings are distinct in both preparation and taste. They are usually smaller, with a thin, silky wrapper and a filling primarily made of minced pork. What sets them apart is the signature sauce—a rich blend of chili oil, garlic, soy sauce, and a hint of sweetness that creates an addictive flavor profile.

Key Characteristics:

  • Texture: The wrappers are thin yet sturdy, allowing the filling to shine.
  • Sauce: A spicy-sweet mix that’s both bold and balanced.
  • Serving Style: Often served without soup, allowing the sauce to coat each dumpling perfectly.

Top Restaurants to Try Zhong Dumplings in Chengdu

1. Long Chao Shou (龙抄手)

A legendary establishment in Chengdu, Long Chao Shou is famous for its dumplings and wontons. While it’s best known for its chaoshou (wontons), their Zhong dumplings are equally outstanding. The restaurant offers a refined version of the dish, with a sauce that’s slightly milder but deeply flavorful.

Why Visit?
- Historic ambiance with traditional Sichuan decor.
- A full menu of other local favorites like dan dan noodles and mapo tofu.

2. Chen Mapo Tofu (陈麻婆豆腐)

Though primarily known for its namesake mapo tofu, this restaurant also serves an excellent version of Zhong dumplings. The sauce here leans more toward the spicy side, making it a great choice for those who love heat.

Pro Tip:
- Pair your dumplings with their iconic mapo tofu for a full Sichuan experience.

3. Zhong’s Dumpling House (钟水饺总店)

This is the place to go for the most authentic Zhong dumplings in Chengdu. As the namesake restaurant, it stays true to the original recipe, with a sauce that’s perfectly balanced—spicy, sweet, and savory all at once.

What to Expect:
- A no-frills, local eatery with quick service.
- Generous portions at affordable prices.

4. Jinli Street Food Stalls

For a more casual experience, head to Jinli Ancient Street, where numerous food vendors serve street-style Zhong dumplings. These versions are often smaller and served on skewers, making them perfect for snacking while exploring the area.

Bonus:
- The lively atmosphere of Jinli Street adds to the experience.
- Try other street foods like lai tang yuan (sweet glutinous rice balls) alongside your dumplings.

5. Yu’s Family Kitchen (玉林餐馆)

A hidden gem in Chengdu’s Yulin district, this family-run spot is beloved by locals. Their Zhong dumplings have a homemade touch, with a slightly thicker wrapper and a sauce that’s less oily but just as flavorful.

Why It’s Worth the Trip:
- Off-the-beaten-path location means fewer tourists.
- Cozy, intimate setting with friendly service.

How to Enjoy Zhong Dumplings Like a Local

The Right Way to Eat Them

  1. Mix Well: Ensure the sauce coats every dumpling evenly.
  2. Pair with Tea: A light jasmine tea helps balance the spice.
  3. Try Different Variants: Some places offer vegetarian or seafood-filled versions.

Best Times to Visit

  • Lunch Hours (11:30 AM - 1:30 PM): Popular spots get crowded, so arrive early.
  • Late Afternoon (3:00 PM - 5:00 PM): A quieter time to enjoy a relaxed meal.

Beyond Dumplings: Other Must-Try Chengdu Dishes

While Zhong dumplings are a highlight, Chengdu’s food scene has much more to offer. Don’t miss:
- Hot Pot (火锅): Sichuan’s famous spicy broth.
- Dan Dan Noodles (担担面): Noodles with a fiery peanut sauce.
- Kung Pao Chicken (宫保鸡丁): A classic stir-fry with a Sichuan twist.

Final Thoughts for Food Travelers

Chengdu is a city where every meal feels like an adventure. Whether you’re indulging in Zhong dumplings at a historic restaurant or grabbing a quick bite from a street vendor, each experience adds to the rich tapestry of Sichuan’s culinary culture. So pack your appetite—and maybe some antacids—because Chengdu’s flavors are bold, unforgettable, and utterly delicious.
