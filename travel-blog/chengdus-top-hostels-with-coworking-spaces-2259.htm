Chengdu’s Top Hostels with Co-Working Spaces

  • /

Chengdu, the capital of Sichuan Province, is a city that effortlessly blends ancient charm with modern vibrancy. Known for its spicy cuisine, laid-back teahouse culture, and of course, the adorable giant pandas, Chengdu has become a hotspot for digital nomads and travelers seeking a unique work-travel balance. If you’re looking for a place to stay that offers both comfort and productivity, here’s a curated list of Chengdu’s top hostels with co-working spaces.

Why Choose a Hostel with a Co-Working Space in Chengdu?

The rise of remote work has transformed the way people travel. No longer confined to traditional vacations, many now combine exploration with productivity. Chengdu, with its affordable living costs, fast internet, and thriving expat community, is an ideal destination for this lifestyle. Hostels with co-working spaces provide the perfect environment:

  • Community Vibes: Meet like-minded travelers and professionals.
  • Cost-Effective: Save money compared to renting a private office or coworking membership.
  • Convenience: Work and stay under one roof, eliminating commute stress.

Top 5 Hostels in Chengdu with Co-Working Spaces

1. The Lazy Panda Hostel & Co-Working Hub

Located in the heart of Chengdu’s Jinli Ancient Street area, The Lazy Panda is a favorite among digital nomads.

Workspace Features

  • High-speed Wi-Fi (100 Mbps)
  • Ergonomic chairs and spacious desks
  • Quiet zones for focused work
  • Free coffee and tea

Why Stay Here?

Beyond work, the hostel organizes weekly Sichuan hotpot nights and panda sanctuary tours. The rooftop lounge offers stunning views of the city skyline, perfect for post-work relaxation.

2. Mingtown Etour International Hostel

A stone’s throw from Chunxi Road shopping district, Mingtown Etour combines affordability with functionality.

Workspace Features

  • 24/7 access to the co-working lounge
  • Printing and scanning services
  • Soundproof phone booths for calls

Why Stay Here?

The hostel’s café serves authentic Sichuan snacks, and its central location makes it easy to explore Chengdu’s nightlife after a productive day.

3. Sleepy Panda’s Co-Living Space

This trendy spot in the Gaoxin district caters to long-term stays, offering private pods and dorms.

Workspace Features

  • Dedicated Zoom rooms for virtual meetings
  • Fast, fiber-optic internet
  • Weekly skill-sharing workshops

Why Stay Here?

Sleepy Panda’s community events, from Mandarin classes to calligraphy workshops, make it a cultural immersion hub.

4. Tripsay Hostel & Co-Working

Nestled near Wide and Narrow Alley (Kuanzhai Xiangzi), Tripsay is ideal for creatives.

Workspace Features

  • Artistic, inspiring décor
  • Outdoor garden workspace
  • High-quality podcasting equipment for rent

Why Stay Here?

The hostel’s partnership with local artists means you’ll often find pop-up galleries and live music events.

5. Base Camp Chengdu

For adventure seekers who need to stay connected, Base Camp near Mount Qingcheng offers a nature-meets-tech retreat.

Workspace Features

  • Solar-powered workspace
  • Hiking breaks with stunning mountain views
  • On-site yoga sessions to recharge

Why Stay Here?

It’s the perfect escape from city chaos while still offering reliable work amenities.

Tips for Digital Nomads in Chengdu

  • Internet Reliability: Most hostels offer strong Wi-Fi, but having a local SIM card (China Mobile/Unicom) as backup is wise.
  • Visa Considerations: Check if your visa allows remote work; many use tourist visas for short stays.
  • Local Eats: Don’t miss out on dan dan noodles and mapo tofu—fuel for productivity!

Chengdu’s hostels with co-working spaces are redefining travel for the modern worker. Whether you’re coding, writing, or designing, these spots ensure you won’t miss a beat while experiencing the best of Sichuan.
